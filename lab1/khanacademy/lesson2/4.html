Travel to Kazakhstan

Almaty

Almaty, the largest city in Kazakhstan, is a vibrant metropolis nestled at the foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau mountains. Formerly the capital of the country until 1997, Almaty continues to be a major cultural, economic, and educational hub. Its name translates to "City of Apples," reflecting the region's rich history of apple cultivation.

Taldyk

Taldykorgan is the administrative center of the Almaty Region in southeastern Kazakhstan. As the regional capital, Taldykorgan plays a crucial role in the economic and administrative landscape of the area. The city is situated in a picturesque setting, surrounded by the Zailisky Alatau mountain range, offering residents and visitors stunning views of the natural beauty that characterizes the region.
